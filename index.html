Indigenous chicken

The term road runner finds special meaning and application in Zimbabwe.It is used to refer to indigenous chicken breeds found in the country.The term road runner is a borrowed one usually refering to a wild bird found in the west.

These roadrunners where traditionally bred and raised in the rural areas, with broiler chicken mainly kept in urban areas. Road runners where a delicacy that most people would have when they visited relatives in the rural areas. The meat is usually served with millet,sorghum and maize pap also known as sadza or isitshwala.

Health benefits of road runners

In recent years there has been a shift in urban dwellers chicken preferences. Road runners are becoming the preferred type of chicken due to their taste and percieved health benefits. Medical information available about broilers indicate that thier consumption is a health risk. This has led health conscious section of citizens to shift from broiler to road runner meat.

Selling Road runners as a business

The shift in preference to road runner meat has seen the business minded people taking advantage of this opportunity. A significant section of both rural and urban dwellers have embarked into the business of selling road runner chicken.

Those who are into breeding and marketing road runners are failing to meet demand. This is due to the fact that road runners are slow to mature taking up to 6 months and this has made it is easy to market road runners.

If venturing unto the business of selling road runners one must carry out research and put together a road runner business plan. Of particular importance is the market research so that the business knows who its target customers are and their location.

Road runner types

There are many types of indigenous chicken in the country. The types include Boschveld,Koekok,Rhode island red and many more. When keeping road runners for business it is advised to make use of breeds such as Boschveld as they have known information about genitic characteristics such as weight, number of eggs laid and more. These characteristics are useful for selection of the type to keep for business purposes.

Road runner farmers coming together

Those into indigenous chicken farming have realised the need to come together and coordinate road runner farming activities. Organisations such as the Zimbabwe Free Range Poultry Producers Association which offer market information and certifications to those who are into indigenous chicken farming have been formed. This shows a paradigm shift in chicken consumption trends in the country. More people are advocating for road runner farming businesses inorder to satisfy demand for their meat.
