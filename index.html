Road runners in Zimbabwe

Indigenous chicken

The term road runner finds special meaning and application in Zimbabwe.It is used to refer to indigenous chicken breeds found in the country.The term road runner is a borrowed one usually refering to a wild bird found in the west.

In Zimbabwe road runners where traditionally bred and raised in the rural areas, with broiler chicken mainly kept in urban areas. Road runners where a delicacy that most people would have when they visited relatives in Zimbabwe's rural areas. The meat is usually served with millet,sorghum and maize pap also known as sadza or isitshwala.

Health benefits of road runners

In recent years there has been a shift in Zimbabwe's urban dwellers chicken preferences. Road runners are becoming the preferred type of chicken due to their taste and percieved health benefits. Medical information available about broilers indicate that thier consumption is a health risk. This has led health conscious section Zimbabweans to shift from broiler to road runner meat.

Road runner business in Zimbabwe

The shift in preference to road runner meat has seen the business minded people taking advantage of this opportunity. A significant section of both rural and urban dwellers in Zimbabwe have embarked into the business of breeding and selling road runner chicken.

The demand for their meat is quite significant with breeders usually failing to meet demand. This is due to the fact that road runners are slow to mature taking up to 6 months to mature.

This means that a healthy market for road runners exists in Zimbabwe. Someone venturing into this business must however do a research and put together a road runner business plan. Of particular importance is the market research so that the business knows who its target customers are and their location.

Road runner types indigenous to Zimbabwe

There are many types of chicken indigenous to Zimbabwe. The types include Boschveld,Koekok,Rhode island red and many more. When keeping road runners for business it is advised to keep these breeds such as Boschveld as they have known information about genitic characteristics such as weight, number of eggs laid and more. These chicken breed characteristics are useful for selection of breed to keep for business purposes.

Road runner farmers coming together in Zimbabwe

Those into indigenous chicken farming have realised the need to come together and coordinate road runner farming activities. Organisations such as the Zimbabwe Free Range Poultry Producers Association which offer market information and certifications to road runner producers have been formed. This shows a paradigm shift in chicken consumption trends in Zimbabwe. More people are advocating for road runner farming businesses inorder to satisfy demand for their meat.
